Brain Box was one of the first things I built in Unity. It was a team project, a learning solution for high school students that administered lessons, helped them revise, and set practice tests. My piece was the revision game, and the brief was open. Make revision something a student would actually choose to do.

So I built a side-scrolling platformer with the revision baked into the run. Quick-fire questions gate each level, and you cannot finish until you answer them, so the student is revising and platforming at the same time. Score rewards speed on both axes. Answer fast, move fast, and the number climbs. Hesitate on a question and the level drags on you.

We entered Brain Box into an island-wide competition and took home an award for most ambitious project. That category is one I have come to read as a kind and accurate way of saying we reached a little past what we could finish cleanly. Which is its own early lesson. But it is the project that got me hooked on Unity, and the first time I watched a single mechanic do real work: turning revision, which nobody looks forward to, into something with a bit of pace and a score attached.

The build on itch.io is the revision game, my part of it. It is a Windows zip. Unzip it and run game.exe.
